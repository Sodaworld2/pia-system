So What's the Right Move?

The research is real. The features are legit. The sprint plan is solid engineering. But there's a gap between building the platform of the future and building what the 9 current users actually need today.

The sweet spot: build the tables and types now (they're cheap), but skip the complex logic until there are real users and real money. A vesting_schedules table costs nothing to create. A full acceleration clause engine with milestone verification is weeks of work for a feature nobody will trigger.

Build the skeleton. Don't furnish every room.

Create the 13 tables (that's just SQL). Skip the complex engines. Ship auth first.
