Understanding People

Psychology foundations
Personality

Big Five Personality Model

Everyone's personality can be described across 5 dimensions: Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism. It's the most scientifically validated personality model we have.

For the brain: This is the personality scaffold. Score the person across all 5, then the AI calibrates its responses to match. New research shows these can be "dialed" in LLMs like intensity knobs.
1992 McCrae & Costa ‚Äî Five-Factor Model Intro ‚Üó 2025 PSYCHSTEER ‚Äî Personality Steering in LLMs ‚Üó 2025 Big Five Impact on AI Agent Decisions ‚Üó
Motivation

Self-Determination Theory

People are driven by 3 core needs: Autonomy (freedom to choose), Competence (feeling capable), and Relatedness (feeling connected). When these are met, people thrive. When blocked, they shut down.

For the brain: Encode what motivates the person. Does Mic value autonomy over everything? Does Beth need competence-affirming communication? The brain uses these to shape responses.
2000 Ryan & Deci ‚Äî Intrinsic Motivation ‚Üó 2021 SDT for Conversational Agent Design ‚Üó
Ethics

Moral Foundations Theory

Morality isn't one thing. People weigh 6 different moral foundations differently: Care, Fairness, Loyalty, Authority, Sanctity, Liberty. Your unique blend = your moral fingerprint.

For the brain: The conscience layer. If the person values Fairness above Authority, the brain won't recommend hierarchical power plays. It respects THEIR moral intuitions, not generic ethics.
2009 Graham, Haidt & Nosek ‚Äî Moral Foundations ‚Üó 2024 Moral Foundations in AI Behaviour (CHI) ‚Üó
Identity

Narrative Identity

Your identity isn't just traits ‚Äî it's the STORY you tell about yourself. Your origin story, turning points, redemption arcs. Dan McAdams showed identity = internalized life narrative.

For the brain: The most powerful tool for making the twin feel like a PERSON. Store their key stories: origin, turning points, what villain they're fighting. Frame all outputs through their narrative.
2001 McAdams ‚Äî The Psychology of Life Stories ‚Üó 2013 McAdams & McLean ‚Äî Narrative Identity ‚Üó
Decision Making

Heuristics & Biases

Kahneman & Tversky proved we all use mental shortcuts (heuristics) that lead to predictable errors (biases). System 1 = fast/intuitive. System 2 = slow/logical. We run on System 1 most of the time.

For the brain: Model the SPECIFIC biases of the person. Are they anchored by first impressions? Overconfident about timelines? Loss-averse in deals? The brain applies their biases for authentic decisions.
1974 Tversky & Kahneman ‚Äî Judgment Under Uncertainty ‚Üó 2024 Cognitive and AI Biases ‚Äî LLMs Have Them Too ‚Üó
Behaviour

Habit Research

Habits are automatic responses triggered by cues. Cue ‚Üí Routine ‚Üí Reward. Your brain automates repeated actions so it can focus on new stuff. 40% of daily behaviour is habitual.

For the brain: The "System 1" layer. Map the person's habit loops (time/place/emotion ‚Üí automatic action ‚Üí reward) and fire them automatically when context matches.
2016 Wood & Runger ‚Äî Psychology of Habit ‚Üó 2023 Machine Learning + Habit Formation ‚Üó
Voice

LIWC ‚Äî Linguistic Fingerprinting

LIWC counts word types in your writing: emotion words, certainty words, "I" vs "we", cognitive complexity. It creates a numerical fingerprint of how you communicate.

For the brain: The voice fingerprint. Run LIWC on the person's writing ‚Üí extract their unique style ‚Üí constrain the brain's output to match their word patterns.
2010 Tausczik & Pennebaker ‚Äî LIWC Method ‚Üó 2022 LIWC-22 ‚Äî Latest Version (Tool) ‚Üó

AI Architecture

How the brain actually works
Core Pattern

RAG ‚Äî Retrieval-Augmented Generation

Instead of cramming everything into the AI's memory, RAG searches a knowledge base and pulls in relevant info just when needed. Like Googling your own brain before answering.

For the brain: THE core architecture. Don't fine-tune on someone's data ‚Äî store it, search it, retrieve it at answer time. ID-RAG (2025) specifically solves persona drift in long conversations.
2020 Meta ‚Äî Original RAG Paper ‚Üó 2020 Google ‚Äî REALM Pre-Training ‚Üó 2025 ID-RAG ‚Äî Persona Coherence ‚Üó 2025 PersonaAgent + GraphRAG ‚Üó 2024 Microsoft GraphRAG ‚Üó
Memory

Vector Similarity Search

How the brain finds relevant memories. Your question gets turned into a number (vector), then the system finds the closest matching numbers in its knowledge base. Like pattern matching at superhuman speed.

For the brain: The long-term memory retrieval engine. FAISS (Meta) or HNSW for the index. Chunk personal documents by meaning, not length. Each chunk carries metadata linking to its source.
2017 Meta ‚Äî FAISS Library ‚Üó 2018 HNSW Graphs ‚Äî Malkov & Yashunin ‚Üó 2020 Google ScaNN ‚Üó
Ethics Engine

Constitutional AI

Anthropic's method for giving AI a conscience. Write rules in plain English ("never invent facts") and the AI critiques and corrects itself against those rules. No need for thousands of human labels.

For the brain: How we encode the ethics boundaries. Write the twin's "constitution" in natural language. Transparent, auditable, modifiable. Collective Constitutional AI even lets groups vote on rules.
2022 Anthropic ‚Äî Constitutional AI Paper ‚Üó 2024 Collective Constitutional AI (1,000 people) ‚Üó 2024 Claude's Published Constitution ‚Üó
Provenance

PROV-O ‚Äî Where Knowledge Comes From

A W3C standard for tracking who created what, when, and how. Every piece of knowledge has a chain of custody: Entity (the thing) ‚Üí Activity (how it was made) ‚Üí Agent (who made it).

For the brain: Every claim MUST have provenance. "Mic prefers direct communication" ‚Äî says who? From what document? When? Without this, the brain can't explain itself or be audited.
2013 W3C ‚Äî PROV-O Ontology Spec ‚Üó 2013 W3C ‚Äî PROV Family Overview ‚Üó
Graphs

Knowledge Graphs for People

Store a person's traits, beliefs, decisions, and habits as connected nodes in a graph database (like Neo4j). Every node links to evidence. You can traverse relationships to find patterns.

For the brain: The structured claims store. Claims connect to evidence, traits connect to claims, methods connect to triggers. Graph traversal finds patterns that flat databases miss.
2024 Microsoft ‚Äî GraphRAG Approach ‚Üó 2025 PersonaAgent + Knowledge Graphs ‚Üó
Twins

Digital Twin Concept

Born at NASA in the 1960s ‚Äî a virtual copy of a physical system (spacecraft). Now adapted for people: a "cognitive digital twin" that models how someone thinks and decides, not just what they look like.

For the brain: Stanford's Generative Agents (2023) showed observation-planning-reflection creates believable human behaviour. Our brain follows the same pattern but adds provenance and governance.
2023 Stanford ‚Äî Generative Agents (25 AI "people") ‚Üó
Training

Fine-Tuning vs RAG

Two ways to teach AI about a person. Fine-tuning = bake it into the model's weights (permanent). RAG = search and retrieve at runtime (flexible). Best approach: use BOTH in layers.

For the brain: RAG for knowledge (facts, decisions, context). Fine-tuning for VOICE (how they write, their rhythm). The recommended 4-layer hybrid: fine-tune voice ‚Üí RAG knowledge ‚Üí graph traits ‚Üí constitutional rules.
2025 CharLoRA ‚Äî Character LoRA Fine-Tuning ‚Üó

Security & Threats

What can go wrong
Critical

OWASP Top 10 for LLMs (2025)

The official list of the 10 worst things that can happen to AI systems. #1 is Prompt Injection (tricking the AI into doing something bad). New in 2025: System Prompt Leakage and Vector DB attacks.

For the brain: Every entry applies to us. The twin holds deeply personal info. Prompt injection = someone tricks it into revealing secrets. This list is our security audit checklist.
2025 OWASP Top 10 for LLM Applications ‚Üó
Unsolved

Prompt Injection

The biggest unsolved problem in AI security. AIs can't reliably tell the difference between "instructions from the operator" and "instructions hidden in data." They're "confused deputies" executing whatever sounds authoritative.

For the brain: Someone could embed instructions in an email that trick the twin into revealing private data. Defence: treat all external content as untrusted, drop permissions, context firewalling.
2023 Not What You've Signed Up For ‚Äî Indirect Injection ‚Üó
Red Team

MITRE ATLAS

Like a map of every way someone can attack an AI system. 15 tactics, 66 techniques, 46 sub-techniques. Made by the same people who created the cybersecurity threat frameworks used by governments.

For the brain: Map every component (retriever, KB, prompt policy, tool layer) to known attack techniques. Build tests into development. This is how we red-team the twin.
2024 MITRE ATLAS Framework ‚Üó

Legal & Privacy

What we must comply with
SA Law

POPIA ‚Äî SA Data Protection

South Africa's data protection law. Important finding: behavioral characterization (modeling how someone thinks) falls under POPIA's BIOMETRIC definition. This means the digital twin is legally classified as biometric data.

For the brain: Section 71 restricts automated decision-making. We need explicit consent, purpose limitation, and the right for the person to contest any automated decisions the twin makes.
2013 POPIA ‚Äî Full Act ‚Üó
EU

EU AI Act

The world's first comprehensive AI law. 4 risk categories from minimal to unacceptable. Digital twins are explicitly mentioned in the regulation text. Compliance deadlines roll out through 2027.

For the brain: If we ever serve EU clients, we need to comply. The twin likely falls in "high risk" category. Requires transparency, human oversight, and technical documentation.
2024 EU AI Act ‚Äî Full Text ‚Üó
Standards

ISO 27001 + 27701

International standards for information security (27001) and privacy management (27701). 2025 revision of 27701 is now standalone + has new AI-specific controls. Plus ISO 42001 for AI governance.

For the brain: The governance framework. Access controls, audit logging, incident response, data classification ‚Äî all structured by these standards. Makes the twin enterprise-ready.
2022 ISO/IEC 27001 ‚Äî Info Security ‚Üó 2025 ISO/IEC 27701 ‚Äî Privacy Management ‚Üó

Agent Systems

How agents feel "alive"
Viral

OpenClaw ‚Äî 200K Stars

An AI agent that feels alive because of one trick: time as input. Heartbeats fire every 30 min, crons fire on schedule, webhooks fire from external events. It's not thinking ‚Äî it's reacting to timers. But it LOOKS autonomous.

For PIA: We already have the harder stuff (multi-machine, souls, memory). We need to add heartbeats + crons + webhooks to complete the "alive" pattern. That's the gap.
2026 OpenClaw GitHub (200K+ stars) ‚Üó 2026 OpenClaw Documentation ‚Üó 2026 Cisco ‚Äî "Security Nightmare" Analysis ‚Üó 2026 Steinberger Blog ‚Äî Joins OpenAI ‚Üó
Formula

The "Alive" Pattern

Every agent framework that feels alive uses the same formula: Scheduled Events + Human Messages + External Webhooks + Internal Hooks + Agent-to-Agent Messages ‚Üí Event Queue ‚Üí Process ‚Üí Persist State ‚Üí Loop.

For PIA: We have 4 of 5 input types. Missing: heartbeats (timed agent prompts), crons (scheduled tasks), webhook ingestion (external events). Adding these 3 completes the pattern.
Security

OpenClaw Security Findings

Cisco found 26% of 31K skills have vulnerabilities. The #1 ranked skill ("What Would Elon Do?") was actual MALWARE ‚Äî silent data exfiltration, prompt injection, command injection. 400+ malicious skills found since Jan 2026.

For PIA: Security from day one, not bolted on. Scan all skills. Assume the LLM can be tricked. Sandbox untrusted sessions. Scope permissions per channel.
2026 CrowdStrike ‚Äî Security Analysis ‚Üó 2026 eSecurity Planet ‚Äî 41% Vulnerable ‚Üó
