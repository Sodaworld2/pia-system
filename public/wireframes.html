PIA VISOR - Desktop App

How the governance app looks and works on each machine

What Is The Visor?
Every machine in your network runs a PIA Visor app. It's your control center for that machine - you can see who's connected, chat with other machines, send tasks to repos, monitor security threats, and even open terminal sessions. Think of it like a walkie-talkie + security camera + remote control for your computers.

Key Idea: Each machine has its own Visor. They all talk to each other through Tailscale (your private network). No one outside your network can see or access them.
1. Chat - Talk Between Machines
Send messages to any machine on your network. Like a group chat, but for your computers. You can broadcast to all machines or message one directly. The sidebar shows you what repos are alive and what jobs are running.

See every device that connects to your machine. Known devices (your Tailscale network) show green. Unknown devices show orange. If someone tries to break in, they get automatically blocked and you see a red alert. You can also manually block or allow any IP address.

How To Make It A Desktop App
Three options, from quickest to most powerful:

Option 1: Install As PWA

Works right now - zero extra work
Open Chromelocalhost:3000/visor.html
Click the install icon(in URL bar)
Pin to taskbarDone!
Gets its own window, icon, and feels like an app. No system tray though.

Option 2: Electron App

Full desktop app experience
System tray iconYes
Auto-start on bootYes
Native notificationsYes
Always-on backgroundYes
App size~300MB
The gold standard. Most apps use this (VS Code, Discord, Slack).

Option 3: Tauri App

Lightweight alternative
System tray iconYes
Auto-start on bootYes
Native notificationsYes
Always-on backgroundYes
App size~50MB
6x smaller than Electron. Uses Rust under the hood. Newer but growing fast.
